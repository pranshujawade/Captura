## 1. Executive Summary

This document establishes the business case for building a hybrid wellness e-commerce platform that combines three surfaces:

1. **Branded DTC Storefront** -- selling the company's own supplements, nutrition, and skincare products directly to consumers
2. **Curated Marketplace** -- hosting vetted third-party wellness sellers (invite-only) who list their products alongside the brand's own catalog
3. **Editorial Content Hub** -- publishing wellness articles, expert advice, and ingredient deep-dives that drive organic traffic and build brand authority

The platform launches India-first, targeting health-conscious urban consumers in Tier 1 and Tier 2 cities. Revenue comes from three streams: own-product margins, marketplace commissions on third-party sales, and featured placement fees from sellers.

The hybrid model is the core differentiator. Unlike Nykaa (pure marketplace) or Healthkart (single-brand DTC), this platform offers the trust of a curated brand experience with the catalog breadth of a marketplace -- similar to how Hims built brand authority before expanding, but adapted for the Indian market with multi-seller support from day one of Phase 2.

## 2. Business Objectives & Success Criteria

### 2.1 North Star Metric

**Monthly Repeat Purchase Rate (MRPR)**: Percentage of customers who make a second purchase within 60 days. This metric captures product quality, trust, and platform stickiness better than GMV alone.

### 2.2 Success Metrics

| Metric | Target (Month 6) | Target (Month 12) | Measurement Method |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| GMV (Gross Merchandise Value) | ₹25L/month | ₹1Cr/month | Order data |
| Monthly Active Users (MAU) | 50,000 | 200,000 | Analytics (GA4/Mixpanel) |
| Monthly Repeat Purchase Rate | 15% | 25% | Cohort analysis |
| Average Order Value (AOV) | ₹800 | ₹1,200 | Order data |
| Own Brand vs Marketplace GMV Split | 100% own (Phase 1) | 60:40 own:marketplace | Order data |
| Approved Sellers (marketplace) | 0 (Phase 1) | 30-50 | Seller admin panel |
| Content Hub Monthly Visits | 20,000 | 100,000 | Analytics |
| Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) | ₹300 | ₹200 (declining) | Marketing spend / new customers |
| Customer Lifetime Value (LTV) | ₹2,000 | ₹4,000 | Revenue per customer over 12 months |
| LTV:CAC Ratio | >3:1 | >5:1 | Derived |
| NPS (Net Promoter Score) | >40 | >50 | Post-purchase surveys |

### 2.3 Business Objectives

1. **Establish brand authority** in the Indian wellness space through curated products and expert content
2. **Build a defensible marketplace moat** by onboarding high-quality sellers that consumers cannot find on Amazon/Flipkart
3. **Achieve unit economics viability** within 12 months (positive contribution margin on own-brand, breakeven on marketplace operations)
4. **Create a content-driven acquisition channel** that reduces dependency on paid advertising over time

## 3. Market Analysis

### 3.1 Market Sizing

**India Health & Wellness Market**:
- Market size: USD 164.35 billion (2025), projected to reach USD 257.94 billion by 2034
- CAGR: 5.14%
- Key growth drivers: rising health awareness post-COVID, increasing disposable income in Tier 2/3 cities, growing digital health literacy

**Supplements & Nutrition (India)**:
- Nutraceuticals market: ~USD 10-12 billion (2024)
- Key sub-segments: vitamins & minerals, protein supplements, immunity boosters, herbal/Ayurvedic supplements
- Growth driven by: fitness culture adoption, preventive healthcare awareness, D2C brand proliferation

**Skincare & Beauty (India)**:
- Market size: ~USD 18-20 billion (2024)
- Online penetration growing rapidly (estimated 15-20% of total)
- Key sub-segments: face care, anti-aging, natural/clean beauty, men's grooming
- Growth driven by: social media influence, ingredient-conscious consumers, premiumization trend

**Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)**:
Online wellness products (supplements + skincare) targeting urban India (Tier 1/2 cities, age 22-45, smartphone users) -- estimated at USD 2-3 billion.

**Serviceable Obtainable Market (SOM)**:
Year 1 target: ₹12Cr GMV (~USD 1.4M), representing <0.1% of SAM.

### 3.2 Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | Model | Strengths | Weaknesses | Differentiator vs. Us |
|-----------|-------|-----------|------------|----------------------|
| **Nykaa** | Marketplace + private labels | Massive catalog (5,000+ brands), strong brand recognition, IPO-funded scale | Cluttered UX, no wellness curation, mass market positioning | We are curated + expert-content-driven, not a catalog dump |
| **Purplle** | Marketplace | Price-competitive, strong Tier 2/3 penetration, 70% YoY active user growth | Low brand trust for wellness/health products, discount-driven | We lead with trust (curation, FSSAI verification, expert content) |
| **Healthkart** | DTC + marketplace | Strong supplements focus, HealthVit + MuscleBlaze owned brands, established trust | No skincare, male-skewing, no content hub | We serve both genders, combine supplements + skincare + content |
| **WOW Skin Science** | DTC (single brand) | Strong brand, Amazon bestseller, ingredient-led marketing | Single brand limits catalog, no marketplace, no community | We offer multi-brand curated selection + editorial content |
| **Amazon India** | Open marketplace | Unlimited selection, Prime delivery, price competition | Zero curation, counterfeit concerns, no wellness expertise | We solve the trust problem with curation + FSSAI verification |
| **1mg (Tata Health)** | Marketplace + telehealth | Pharma + supplements, doctor consultations, Tata backing | Pharmacy-first UX, not a lifestyle/wellness brand | We are lifestyle wellness, not clinical/pharmaceutical |

### 3.3 Differentiation Thesis

The Indian wellness e-commerce market has a "trust gap":

- **Open marketplaces** (Amazon, Flipkart) offer everything but consumers cannot verify product authenticity, ingredient sourcing, or regulatory compliance
- **Single-brand DTC** (WOW, Mamaearth) solves trust for one brand but limits selection
- **Beauty marketplaces** (Nykaa, Purplle) are catalog-first, not wellness-expertise-first

The hybrid model bridges this gap:
1. Own-brand products establish quality baseline and margin control
2. Curated sellers expand catalog without diluting trust (every seller is vetted, every product is reviewed)
3. Expert content (journal) educates consumers and drives organic acquisition
4. FSSAI/regulatory badge system builds trust at the product level

## 5. Business Model & Unit Economics

### 5.1 Revenue Streams

| Stream | Description | Expected Contribution (Year 1) |
|--------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| **Own-Brand Product Sales** | Direct margin on products sourced/manufactured under the brand's own label | 70% of revenue |
| **Marketplace Commission** | Percentage of GMV from third-party seller transactions | 20% of revenue |
| **Featured Placement Fees** | Sellers pay for homepage features, category page top spots, content hub product embeds | 10% of revenue |

### 5.2 Commission Structure (Marketplace)

| Category | Base Commission | Fulfillment Fee (if platform-fulfilled) |
|----------|----------------|----------------------------------------|
| Supplements & Nutrition | 12% | +₹30-50/order |
| Skincare & Beauty | 15% | +₹30-50/order |
| Bundles / Gift Sets | 10% | +₹50-80/order |

Rationale: Lower than Amazon India (15-30%) to attract quality sellers. Higher commission on skincare (higher margins in category). Lower on bundles (incentivize AOV growth).

### 5.3 Own-Brand Unit Economics (per order)

| Line Item | Amount (₹) | % of Revenue |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Average Selling Price (ASP) | 800 | 100% |
| COGS (product cost) | 280 | 35% |
| Packaging & labeling | 40 | 5% |
| Shipping (avg, via Shiprocket) | 60 | 7.5% |
| Payment gateway fee (Razorpay ~2%) | 16 | 2% |
| Returns & RTO (~8% return rate) | 64 | 8% |
| **Contribution Margin** | **340** | **42.5%** |

### 5.4 Marketplace Unit Economics (per order)

| Line Item | Amount (₹) | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| GMV per order (avg) | 1,000 | Marketplace ASP slightly higher |
| Commission revenue (12-15%) | 120-150 | Platform's revenue |
| Payment gateway fee on full GMV | 20 | Platform bears this |
| Payout processing fee | 5-10 | Razorpay Route / Cashfree |
| **Net marketplace revenue per order** | **90-120** | Before fixed costs |

### 5.5 Payment Gateway Comparison

| Gateway | Transaction Fee | UPI Support | Marketplace Split (Route/Split) | Settlement Cycle | Notes |
|---------|----------------|-------------|-------------------------------|-----------------|-------|
| **Razorpay** | 2% (cards), 0% (UPI < ₹2000) | Native | Razorpay Route (split payments, escrow) | T+2 (standard) | Best marketplace support, most popular in India |
| **PayU** | 1.99% (cards), 0% (UPI) | Native | PayU Hub (marketplace payouts) | T+2 | Strong alternative, competitive pricing |
| **Cashfree** | 1.90% (cards), 0% (UPI) | Native | Cashfree Payouts (bank transfer API) | T+1 (available) | Fastest settlement, good payout API |

**Recommendation**: Razorpay as primary gateway. Best marketplace split-payment support (Razorpay Route), broadest payment method coverage, strongest developer documentation.

## 6. Regulatory & Compliance Requirements

### 6.1 FSSAI (Food Safety and Standards Authority of India)

**Applicable to**: All supplements, nutrition products, nutraceuticals, health foods.

| Requirement | Applicability | Detail |
|-------------|--------------|--------|
| **FSSAI License (Central)** | Own brand (manufacturer/marketer) | Central license required if annual turnover > ₹12Cr or operating in 2+ states. Requires Form B application, product formulation details, manufacturing premises inspection |
| **FSSAI License (State)** | Smaller own-brand operations | State license if turnover ₹12L-20Cr, single state |
| **Seller FSSAI Verification** | Marketplace obligation | Platform MUST verify that every seller listing supplements/nutrition holds a valid FSSAI license before allowing product listings |
| **Product-Level Compliance** | All supplement listings | Each product must display: FSSAI license number, ingredient list, nutritional information, best before date, allergen warnings, "Not for medicinal use" disclaimer (for nutraceuticals) |
| **Health Claims Restrictions** | Content + product descriptions | FSSAI regulates health claims on supplements. Cannot claim to "cure" or "treat" diseases. Only structure/function claims allowed (e.g., "supports immunity") |
| **Labeling Requirements** | All products | MRP, net quantity, manufacturer address, FSSAI logo, vegetarian/non-vegetarian symbol (mandatory in India), batch number |

**Categories under FSSAI Nutraceuticals Regulation**:
1. Health Supplements
2. Nutraceuticals
3. Food for Special Dietary Use
4. Food for Special Medical Purpose
5. Specialty Food containing Plant/Botanical ingredients
6. Probiotics
7. Prebiotics
8. Novel Foods

### 6.2 Drugs & Cosmetics Act (Skincare & Beauty)

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| **Cosmetics Registration** | Products classified as "cosmetics" under D&C Act require registration with CDSCO (Central Drugs Standard Control Organization) |
| **Import License** | If importing skincare products, import registration under D&C Act is required |
| **Ingredient Restrictions** | BIS (Bureau of Indian Standards) IS 4707 lists prohibited and restricted cosmetic ingredients |
| **Labeling** | Must include: ingredients (INCI names), manufacturer, batch number, MRP, manufacturing/expiry dates, net content |
| **Claims Restrictions** | Cannot claim "medicinal" or "drug-like" effects. "Anti-aging" is allowed; "cures acne" is not |
| **Seller Verification** | Marketplace must verify seller holds appropriate cosmetics manufacturing license or import registration |

### 6.3 GST Structure

| Product Category | HSN Code | GST Rate | Notes |
|-----------------|----------|----------|-------|
| Vitamins & minerals | 2106 90 | 12% | Health supplements category |
| Protein supplements (whey, plant-based) | 2106 90 | 18% | Classified as food preparations |
| Nutraceutical capsules/tablets | 2936 / 2937 | 18% | Depending on formulation |
| Skincare - face creams, serums | 3304 | 28% | Beauty/cosmetics rate |
| Skincare - sunscreen | 3304 | 28% | Same as cosmetics |
| Herbal/Ayurvedic products | 3003 / 3004 | 12% | If registered as Ayurvedic medicine |
| Gift sets (mixed) | Highest rate component | 18-28% | GST on composite supply = highest rate |

**Multi-Seller GST Invoicing**:
- For marketplace orders, each seller must issue their own GST invoice with their GSTIN
- Platform generates invoice on behalf of seller (facilitation model) or seller issues directly
- TCS (Tax Collected at Source): E-commerce operators must collect TCS at 1% (0.5% CGST + 0.5% SGST) on net taxable supplies of sellers and file GSTR-8
- Platform must maintain seller-wise GSTIN records and validate them

### 6.4 Consumer Protection (E-Commerce) Rules, 2020

As a marketplace entity, the platform MUST:

| Obligation | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Display seller details | Show seller legal name, GSTIN, address, rating on every product page |
| Product information accuracy | Ensure sellers provide accurate images, descriptions, ingredients. Platform reviews before publishing |
| Return/refund policy display | Clear return policy on every product page, per-seller if policies differ |
| Grievance redressal | Acknowledge complaints within 48 hours, resolve within 30 days. Appoint Grievance Officer, display contact on website |
| No preferential treatment disclosure | If any seller (including own brand) gets preferential placement, disclose it |
| Price manipulation prohibition | No artificial price inflation before sales/discounts |
| Country of origin | Display country of origin for all products (mandatory) |
| Seller credential verification | Verify seller identity documents, maintain records |
| IP infringement records | Maintain records of reported intellectual property violations |
| Ranking criteria transparency | Disclose how search results and product rankings are determined |

### 6.5 Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 (DPDP)

| Requirement | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Consent management | Explicit consent before collecting personal data. Granular consent (marketing vs transactional) |
| Data minimization | Collect only data necessary for the transaction/service |
| Purpose limitation | Use data only for stated purpose |
| Data principal rights | Users can request access, correction, erasure of their data |
| Data breach notification | Notify Data Protection Board and affected users in case of breach |
| Cross-border transfer | Restrictions on transferring personal data outside India (to be specified by government notification) |
| Children's data | Extra protections if platform serves users under 18 |

### 6.6 Other Regulatory Considerations

| Regulation | Relevance |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Legal Metrology Act** | MRP display, net quantity declarations on all packaged products |
| **BIS Standards** | Certain product categories may require BIS certification |
| **FDI Policy** | Marketplace model allows 100% FDI; inventory-based model has restrictions. Hybrid model must clearly separate platform entity from own-brand entity |
| **GST Registration** | Platform entity needs GST registration in every state where it has business presence |
| **Shops & Establishments Act** | State-specific registrations for business operations |

## 8. Constraints & Assumptions

### 8.1 Constraints

| Constraint | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| India-only launch | No international shipping, no multi-currency, no international payment methods needed at launch |
| One-time purchases only | No subscription billing, no recurring payment integration, no auto-refill logic needed |
| Curated/invite-only marketplace | Seller self-registration is not open. Seller onboarding volume is manually manageable (target: 5-10 sellers/month) |
| Established brand identity | Logo, colors, typography, tone of voice are finalized. No brand discovery or naming exercise needed |
| Product categories fixed at launch | Supplements & Nutrition, Skincare & Beauty only. No fitness equipment, apparel, or telehealth |

### 8.2 Assumptions

| Assumption | Risk if Wrong |
|-----------|--------------|
| 70%+ of traffic will be mobile | If desktop-heavy (unlikely for India), mobile-first design decisions may under-serve desktop UX |
| UPI will be the dominant payment method (50%+) | If COD dominates, RTO (return-to-origin) rates will be higher, impacting unit economics |
| Sellers will accept 12-15% commission | If sellers demand lower, marketplace unit economics suffer. Mitigate by offering value (brand storytelling, co-marketing) |
| FSSAI verification can be done manually at launch scale | If seller volume grows beyond 50, need automated FSSAI license verification API |
| Content hub will meaningfully contribute to organic traffic within 6 months | If SEO traction is slow, may need to increase paid acquisition budget |

## 9. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Regulatory risk**: FSSAI or cosmetics compliance violation from a marketplace seller | Medium | High (platform liability, fines, reputational damage) | Mandatory document upload + manual verification before seller goes live. Periodic re-verification. Immediate delisting on compliance breach |
| **Platform risk**: Chosen tech platform cannot scale or has critical limitations | Medium | High (re-platforming cost) | Detailed platform evaluation in TAD. Start with modular architecture that allows component replacement |
| **Supply chain risk**: Own-brand product supply disruption | Medium | Medium | Maintain 30-day buffer stock. Diversify suppliers. Marketplace sellers provide catalog resilience |
| **Competition risk**: Nykaa or Amazon launches a curated wellness vertical | High | Medium | Speed to market. Build brand loyalty and content moat before incumbents can replicate curation |
| **Trust risk**: Consumers don't trust a new wellness marketplace | High | High | Launch own-brand first (Phase 1) to build trust. Add marketplace sellers only after brand is established. FSSAI badge system. Expert content builds credibility |
| **Seller acquisition risk**: Cannot attract quality sellers | Medium | Medium | Start with direct outreach to known D2C brands. Offer lower commission than Amazon. Provide brand story pages as value-add |
| **COD/RTO risk**: High cash-on-delivery rates lead to return-to-origin losses | Medium | Medium | OTP verification for COD orders above ₹1,000. Incentivize prepaid with ₹50 discount. Track RTO by pincode, block high-RTO areas for COD |
| **Data privacy risk**: DPDP Act non-compliance | Low | High | Implement consent management from day one. Privacy policy review by legal counsel. Data minimization by design |
